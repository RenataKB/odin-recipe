Tuna Sandwich

Description

Here's my classic tuna sandwich. Tuna in oil, not water. Juice from the pickle jar adds tang - oh, and let's use the pickles too! Dijon for flavour, dill for lovely herbiness, celery for crunch and green onion for freshness. Spread onto your favourite bread and enjoy!

Maybe there are great tuna sandwiches out there. But why risk it when you can eat a sure thing at home, adding zing and freshness to transform fish out of a can into a sandwich filling so good you'll want to use it as a dip for dunking? (Oh yes I do!)

This recipe (text and images) are from this site.

Ingredients

Tuna sandwich filling

  • 425g/ 15 oz canned tuna in oil, drained
  • 150 g whole egg mayonnaise or Kewpie
  • 3/4 cup finely diced celery
  • 50 g finely chopped dill pickle
  • 2 tbsp liquid from pickle jar
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 3 tbsp finely chopped dill (or parsley or basil)
  • 25 g finely sliced green onion
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

For sandwiches

  • 8 pieces butter lettuce (2 per sandwich)
  • Salted butter, for spreading
  • 8 slices bread or 4 rolls

Steps

  1. Filling - Put all the Tuna Filling ingredients in a bowl. Mix well using a wooden spoon, breaking up the tuna so the filling becomes fairly smooth. The pickles and celery will take a beating too which is encouraged -> flavour melding!
  2. Sandwich - Divide Tuna Filling between 4 sandwiches (or more/less depending on bread size). I butter the bread and use 2 pieces of lettuce per sandwich. Enjoy!
